AI-Powered TEM Catalyst Particle Analyzer

A desktop application to automate nanoparticle size analysis from TEM images.

Manual particle size analysis from Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) images is a major bottleneck in materials researchâ€”it's time-consuming, subjective, and doesn't scale. To solve this, I developed a user-friendly application that leverages an AI model to automate the detection and measurement of catalyst nanoparticles.

Core Features

  • Automated Particle Detection: Utilizes a custom-trained computer vision model to identify and segment nanoparticles with high accuracy.
  • Batch Processing & Data Export: Analyze entire folders of images at once and instantly export size distribution data and histograms for reports.
  • Interactive GUI: Provides a simple interface for users to load images, run the analysis, and review the results.

This tool transforms a tedious manual task into a rapid, accurate, and reproducible workflow, accelerating the pace of catalyst research and development.

Ph.D. Research

Optimizing Carbon Nanotube Synthesis via Multiphysics Modeling

Developing computational models to enhance Floating Catalyst Chemical Vapor Deposition (FCCVD).

My doctoral research focuses on optimizing the synthesis of Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) in FCCVD reactors. By developing multi-physics computational models in OpenFOAM and COMSOL, I simulate the complex interplay of fluid dynamics, heat transfer, mass transfer, and aerosol dynamics within the reactor.

These model-guided insights allow for targeted experimental design and process optimization. A key achievement has been engineering a custom Deep Injection FCCVD setup, which, validated by my computational predictions, led to a 30% increase in carbon conversion during material synthesis.

This work combines fundamental science with practical engineering to create more efficient and scalable methods for producing advanced materials.
